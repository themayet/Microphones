Handheld

As the name suggests, a handheld does require the present or performaer to hold it, but can be used in conjunction with the microphone stand for hands-free operation or to amplify musical instruments. The handheld mic is commonplaceand there’s a familiar comfort in using one, but it can be prone to improper usage. As a presenter’s hand moves, so does the mic resulting in volume fluctuations, To keep volume and sound quality consistent, try and maintain 1-2 inches of space betwwen your mouth and mic.

Lavalier

A lavalier (a.k.a lav mic, lapel mic, or clip-on mic) is a small microphone that clips on to clothing. The spot to clip it is along the shirt placket or on a tie. This centre position ensures sound is picked up when the presenter’s head turns. A thin cord runs from the mic to a small transmitter concealed under clothing or clipped to a belt. Lavalier mics are easy to exchange between presenters, but can be challenging to place on certain types of clothing like dresses or t-shirts. Lavalier mics are usually black, which may stand out against light colors, but specialty colors can be found.

Headset

Like the lavalier mic, a headset microphone is hands-free with hidden transmitter components, but instead of affixed to clothing, a headset mic is affixed nearer the mouth to ensure crystal clear audio with greatly reduced ambient noise. The headset mic is looped over one or both ears with a slim rod extending towards the mouth, where the microphone sits. Depending on the style of headset, a small piece of tape may be applied to the cheek/jawline to secure the microphone in place.
